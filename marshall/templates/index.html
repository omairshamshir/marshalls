Complaints Procedure

At Edward Marshall Solicitors we strive to give our clients a satisfactory service. However, in the unlikely event you have any complaints about the service you have received we have the following procedures in place


Informal Stage

For minor complaints we would suggest you complete our Client Care Questionnaire. Your complaint will be handled immediately by the person in charge of your service. Should you remain unsatisfied you may request that the matter be referred to the Partner in charge of the relevant office and, hence, move to Formal Stage 1.


Formal Stage 1

For the avoidance of any doubt or future disputes it is required the complaint is put in writing to the Partner in charge of the relevant office who will investigate the matter and respond to you as quickly as possible. Should at that stage you remain unsatisfied, you may request that the matter be referred to the Partner in charge of another office for independent review and handling, in accordance with Formal Stage 2.


Formal Stage 2

The Partner in charge of the chosen office will review the complaint independently and respond to you as quickly as possible, which may take a little longer than in stage 1. If at that stage the client remains unsatisfied, then they should refer the matter to the LCS (Legal Complaints Service).


Timing

Under formal Stage 1 and/or Formal Stage 2 we will use every endevour to respond to you within 7 days. If for any reason we are unable to do so we will write to tell you why and advise you of a revised timing.


Legal Complaints Service

If you are not satisfied with our handling of your complaint you can ask the Legal Ombudsman (address PO Box 15870, Birmingham, B30 9EB, website www.legalombudsman.org.uk, telephone 0300 555 0333) to consider the complaint. Normally, you will need to bring a complaint to the Legal Ombudsman within six months of receiving a final written response from us about your complaint.


Code of Conduct

The service Edward Marshall Solicitors offers is provided by Solicitors of England and Wales and is regulated by the Solicitors' Regulation Authority (SRA). You can view the Authority's rules by clicking the following link: www.sra.org.uk/code-of-conduct.page
